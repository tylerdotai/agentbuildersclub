# ClawPlex Facebook Group Research Tool

## What This Does
Visits Facebook groups directly, scrapes recent post engagement data (comments, reactions), and ranks groups by actual activity — not member count.

## How To Use
When you want fresh group research, ask me:
```
@Dexter research Facebook groups for ClawPlex. Focus on DFW / Texas entrepreneurs, AI builders, small business owners. Find groups where posts get 10+ comments consistently. Prioritize groups with active discussion culture. Report back with group name, URL, and 2-3 examples of recent active posts.
```

## Better Prompts For Better Results

### Basic
```
Research DFW Facebook groups for [TOPIC]. Find active groups where people comment on posts. 
Ignore follower counts. Focus on: who's actually talking?
```

### Detailed
```
Find Facebook groups for [YOUR NICHE] in [YOUR CITY/REGION]. 
For each group I need:
1. Group name + URL
2. What the engagement looks like (do posts get comments? how many?)
3. What kind of people are in it (builders? hustle culture? local business?)
4. What's the posting style like (promotional? discussion? questions?)

Ignore: Groups with 0 comment activity. Groups that are just promo boards.
Priority: Groups where people actually converse.
```

### For ClawPlex-Specific Outreach
```
Find Facebook groups where DFW builders, indie hackers, and AI tool users actually hang out.
Look for groups with:
- Posts about building products, not just "check out my stuff"
- Questions about AI tools, no-code, local models, agents
- People sharing what they shipped
- Comment threads on posts (not just reactions)

Target: 10-20 groups with active discussion. Give me URLs and specific engagement examples.
```
